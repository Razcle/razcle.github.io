Revision Notes 1 - Inference in the Ising Model

By Raza Habib May 25, 2017

The plan

It’s been roughly a year now since I finished my masters in machine learning at UCL. It was an excellent course that I thoroughly enjoyed and covered an enormous range of different topics. Since starting the PhD I’ve been more focussed on a narrower set of problems but I dont want to lose the knowledge that I gained during my masters that I’m not using right at this moment. To try and keep fresh, I’ve decided to revise some of the material from my courses and make a few revision notes for myself.

Im going to post my notes on here in case they might be useful to others. Topics that I’d like to recap include:

  • Mean Field Variational Inference
  • Variational Bayes
  • Sparse GP approximations
  • The Gaussian Process Latent Variable Model
  • Loopy Belief propagation
  • Expectation propogation

I suspect that it’ll take me a long time to cover all of these and my primary focus is research but whenever I do have time, I’ll try to make some good notes on these topics and stick them here. They’ll generally be quite brief since they are meant to be revision summaries rather than full explanations.

To start with, I’m going to make some notes on inference in Ising models.
